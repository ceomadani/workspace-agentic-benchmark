# Pillar 6 · Observability & Recovery · L4 Optimizing

## What the audit detected
A live registry of active entities, a liveness/health watchdog, and scheduled aggregate reporting.

## Substantiating artifacts
- `00_ENTITY-REGISTRY.md` · single source of truth for active daemons, cron jobs, tools, hooks — auto-regenerated by `11_TOOLS/build-entity-registry.sh`.
- `11_TOOLS/liveness-watchdog.py` · 6-state health model (M08) over the agentic daemons.
- `11_TOOLS/aggregate-report.py` · daily cron (09:00) rolling up status.
- launchd cron fleet (reflexion 23:30 · dreams 03:00 · hermes-stale 02:30 · memory-promote 04:00 · aggregate-report 09:00 · liveness-watchdog) loaded with exit-code tracking.

## Operator commentary
The registry is regenerable from the filesystem, so observability survives drift — and the
SessionStart hook surfaces daemon/cron health + exit codes at cold start. Recovery runbooks and
the watchdog's state machine push this to L4. (One known gap: `hermes-stale` recently exited 1 —
visible precisely because the observability layer reports it.)
